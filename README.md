## Folder Structure

The project contains two folders:

### `Verses`

Contains all **6,236** verse audio files.

Each filename follows this format:

```text
SSSAAA.mp3
```

Where:

- **SSS** = Surah number (three digits)
- **AAA** = Ayah number (three digits)

Examples:

- `001001.mp3` → Surah 1, Ayah 1
- `002255.mp3` → Surah 2, Ayah 255
- `114004.mp3` → Surah 114, Ayah 4

This naming convention makes the collection easy to integrate into applications and scripts.

### `Basmala`

This folder contains **seven** recordings of the Basmala:

- `B1.mp3`
- `B2.mp3`
- `B3.mp3`
- `B4.mp3`
- `B5.mp3`
- `B6.mp3`
- `B7.mp3`

These recordings contain only:

> **Bismillahir Rahmanir Raheem**

They are provided separately so applications may choose whichever version they prefer or randomly select one at the beginning of each surah.

> **Important:** The Basmala should **not** be played before **Surah At-Tawbah (Surah 9)**, as that surah begins directly with its first verse and is traditionally recited without the Basmala.

Both the **64 kbps** and **128 kbps** editions include the same seven Basmala recordings.

## About the Recitation

This collection is based on **Sheikh Muhammad Siddiq Al-Minshawi's New Murattal** recitation.

In 1965, Sheikh Al-Minshawi completed recording a full Murattal Quran for the Holy Quran Radio in Cairo. Although the recordings were officially approved, he was not fully satisfied with parts of his performance. He later requested that portions of the recording be recorded again, and the revised recordings were officially approved in 1967.

The version commonly known today as the **New Murattal** combines these revised recordings with the remaining original recordings to form the complete Quran.

---

## About Sheikh Muhammad Siddiq Al-Minshawi

**Sheikh Muhammad Siddiq Al-Minshawi** was born in **1920** in Egypt and passed away on **20 June 1969** at the age of 49.

He is widely regarded as one of the greatest Quran reciters of the twentieth century. His precise tajweed, beautiful voice, and heartfelt style continue to inspire millions of Muslims around the world.

*May Allah have mercy upon him.*
